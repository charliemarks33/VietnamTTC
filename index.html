The War That Never Ended

Exploring the emotional aftermath of Vietnam

Kiowa's Death Tribal Brotherhood After the Crusade Tet Offensive

“That night when Kiowa got wasted...”

“That night when Kiowa got wasted, I sort of sank down into the sewage with him.” (The Things They Carried, Chapter 16, Page 150)

This quote captures the guilt and emotional drowning that many soldiers experienced after losing close friends in combat.

“You become part of a tribe...”

“You become part of a tribe and you share the same blood.” (The Things They Carried, Chapter 20)

This illustrates the deep brotherhood formed between soldiers, a bond so strong it replaces the life they had before the war.

“The last battle ended 20 years ago...”

“The last battle ended 20 years ago, but if the end of a war is peace, we’re still waiting for it.” (Tobias Wolff, After the Crusade)

Veterans carried the psychological scars of war long after the physical battles were over. Many never truly found peace.

“Is it possible to win a battle...”

“Is it possible to win a battle and lose the war?” (Lessons from the Tet Offensive)

The Tet Offensive showed how even tactical victories could become moral and strategic defeats, shaking public support and soldier morale.
