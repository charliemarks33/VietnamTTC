The War That Never Ended

How the Vietnam War followed soldiers home

Coming Home—But Never the Same

For many Vietnam veterans, returning home didn’t mean peace. It meant silence, misunderstanding, and memories too heavy to carry. The war was over—but the battle within was just beginning.

“I came back to a world that didn’t know me. I left part of myself in the jungle, and what returned… wasn’t whole.”

— Vietnam veteran, 1974

Timeline of Invisible Wounds

1969: Lost a Best Friend

A fellow soldier was killed by a landmine. Survivors often blamed themselves—called “survivor’s guilt.”

1971: Numbness Sets In

Repeated trauma led to emotional shutdown. Some veterans described it as "watching your soul rot from the inside."

1975: Return Without a Welcome

Many came home to protest signs, not parades. They buried their pain in silence, often untreated.

1982: The Wall Is Built

The Vietnam Veterans Memorial was dedicated. For many, it was the first time they felt seen again.

“There were nights I screamed without knowing why. Days I couldn’t leave the house. I was still in Vietnam, even in my sleep.”

— Anonymous letter to the VA, 1980s
